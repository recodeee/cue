Diagnosis & treatment guideline

Acne is a chronic inflammatory skin disease of the pilosebaceous unit, common in adolescence and primarily affecting the face. Cross-sectional statistics in the Chinese population put the incidence rate at 8.1% [1]. But research finds that >95% of people will experience varying degrees of acne; 3–7% of acne patients are left with scarring, significantly impacting patients' physical and psychological health. Clinicians' choice of acne treatment varies widely; some treatments have uncertain efficacy and lack evidence-based support, and individual approaches even harm patients. It is therefore essential to formulate effective acne-treatment guidelines that provide diagnostic and therapeutic guidance for clinicians at all levels and standardize treatment.
